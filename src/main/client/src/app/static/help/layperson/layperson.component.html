In clinical settings, phenotypic abnormalities are often defined using medical terminology, which can be difficult for patients to understand. This so-called terminology gap between can impede participation of patients and families in research projects concerned with the phenotype or natural history of diseases, and can even make it difficult for patients to understand data from their own medical charts.

The HPO Project (as part of the Monarch Initiative) has therefore "translated" the terms of the HPO into "plain language". For instance, the term for Macrocephaly can be translated as Large skull.
