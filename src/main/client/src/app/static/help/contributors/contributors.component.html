Contributors to the Human Phenotype Ontology

The Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) has been under development since 2008 and has benefited enormously from a team-science approach under the auspices of the Monarch Initiative, establish in 2009. From the beginning, the HPO has worked with clinicians to improve, refine, and extend areas of the HPO to meet the needs of clinicians and translational researchers. The first such workshop was conducted in 2009 at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute with Professors Helen Firth and Nigel Carter. This page represents an incomplete list of all those who have made contributions to the HPO (please let us know about any omissions!).

OMIM

The initial version of the HPO was constructed in 2008 using a file from the Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man (OMIM) resource by extracting the textual descriptions of each disease as listed in OMIM’s Clinical Synopsis section. The HPO was constructed using in-house scripts and Java programs as well as the ontology editing tool OBO Edit by manually curating all items that occured twice or more in the entire OMIM corpus. We merged synonyms into unique HPO terms. For instance, the three OMIM descriptions Carpal bone hypoplasia, Hypoplasia of carpal bones, and Hypoplastic carpal bones were fused into the single term HP:0001498, Carpal bone hypoplasia. Domain knowledge was also used to develop a subclass hierarchy and to define the necessary general terms, such as Aplasia/hypoplasia of the outer ear, to group more specific terms, as well as to define links between individual terms. Details about the initial construction of the HPO can be found in the initial publication. We would like to acknowledge countless constructive interactions with the OMIM team since 2008.

The Phenotype and Trait Ontology Project

The teams in Berlin and in the UK and US first joined forces in 2009 under the auspices of an NIH-funded project that we call the “PATO grant” (2009-2014). Here we laid the foundation for the computational logic that underlies the HPO, Mammalian Phenotype Ontology, and zebrafish phenotype annotations by developing a scheme for creating logical definitions of individual phenotypic abnormalities. The framework is based on a number of computational resources include the Phenotype and Trait Ontology (PATO) and the Uberon cross-species anatomy ontology. This framework allows not only computational quality control of the ontologies and annotations but also enables cross-species phenotypic comparisons.

IRDiRC

The International Rare DIseases Research Consortium aims to enable all people living with a rare disease to receive an accurate diagnosis, care, and available therapy within one year of coming to medical attention. The HPO utilized by almost all member countries and has been a key source of collaboration, validation, and community dissemination. HPO has therefore been adopted as a Recommended Resource for use in rare disease diagnostics by the IRDiRC. Workshops

This is a partial list of workshops (“hackathons”) conducted over the last decade to add content to the HPO for specific areas of medicine. Please contact the HPO team if you would like to work with us on HPO terms for an area of medicine.

  • 2017: Workshop on Ophthalmology phenotypes and rare diseases of the European Reference Network for Rare Eye Diseases
  • 2017: Workshop on cardiology at Stanford University coordinated by Matthew Wheeler
  • 2017: Workshop on Immunology terms at the National Institute for Allergy and Infectious Diseases coordinated by Morgan Similuk
  • 2015: Workshop with the UK's National Institute for Health Research (NIHR) Rare Disease initiatives on projects such as NIHR RD-TRC (Rare Disease—Translational Research Collaboration, http://rd.trc.nihr.ac.uk) coordinated by Patrick Chinnery, Mareike Veltman and Roger James.
  • 2013: Workshop on epilepsy phenotypes and EEG abnormalities led by Prof. Ingo Helbig (currently Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia, University of Pennsylvania)
  • 2012-2016: Work on bleeding phenotypes with contributions from multiple members of a team led by Prof. Willem Ouwehand of the University of Cambridge (see https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25949529)
  • 2012: 2-day workshop in Berlin, Germany, to extend dental terminology in the HPO led by Prof. Isabelle Bailleul-Forestier (Paul Sabatier University - Toulouse, France)
  • 2009: 5-day workshop at the Sanger Institute with UK clinicians involved with the DECIPHER project
